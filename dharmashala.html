About

Dharamshala is a city in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. Surrounded by cedar forests on the edge of the Himalayas, this hillside city is home to the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile. The Thekchen Chöling Temple Complex is a spiritual center for Tibetan Buddhism, while the Library of Tibetan Works and Archives houses thousands of precious manuscripts.

Nearby attractions:


  • Bhagsunag Falls

    • Bhagsunag Falls is located in the city of McLeod Ganj, a suburb of Dharamshala in the Kangra district of Himachal Pradesh, India. Known as "Little Lhasa" due to its large population of Tibetans, McLeod Ganj is a popular tourist destination for its blend of Indian and Tibetan cultures, serene landscapes, and spiritual tranquillity.

    Bhagsunag Falls, also known as Bhagsu Falls, is a beautiful waterfall that cascades down a height of about 20 metres. The falls are named after the Bhagsunag Temple, an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located nearby. The waterfall and the surrounding area are steeped in mythological significance, with locals believing that the waterfall has healing properties.

    Best Time to Visit Bhagsunag Falls

    The best time to visit the waterfall is during the monsoon season (July to September), when the waterfall is in full flow.

    How to Reach Bhagsunag Falls

    The waterfall can be reached by a short trek from McLeod Ganj. McLeod Ganj is well-connected by road to other major cities in Himachal Pradesh.

    Tips for Visiting Bhagsunag Falls

    Visitors are advised to carry water and snacks. Also, be cautious while walking near the waterfall as the rocks can be slippery.

    Nearby Tourist Spots of Bhagsunag Falls

    Nearby tourist spots include the Bhagsunag Temple, the Dalai Lama Temple, and the Tsuglagkhang Complex. The bustling markets of McLeod Ganj, known for their Tibetan handicrafts, are also a popular attraction.

  • The Kangra Fort

    • Kangra is a picturesque city located in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh, approximately 460 km north of Delhi. The name 'Kangra' is derived from the word 'Kangaradesh', which is mentioned in the ancient Indian scriptures. Kangra is famous for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty, including the majestic Dhauladhar mountain range.

    Best time to visit Kangra Fort

    The best time to visit is during the cooler months of October to March.

    How to reach Kangra Fort

    The fort is easily accessible by road from Kangratown. The nearest railway station is Pathankot, about 87 km away, and the nearest airport is Gaggal, about 13 km away.

    Tips for Visiting Kangra Fort, Kangra

    Remember to carry water and wear sunscreen as the fort area can get quite hot. Also, hiring a guide can enhance your understanding of the fort's history.

    Nearby tourist spots of Kangra Fort

    Nearby attractions include the Masroor Rock Cut Temple, the Kangra Art Museum, and the Kareri Lake

  • St. John Church

    • The church is located amidst deodar forests, and is built in neo-Gothic architectural style. Don’t miss the famous Belgian stained-glass windows here.

How to reach:

Reaching the magnificent hill station of Dharamshala is not a hassle for travellers coming from various parts of India. Delhi and Punjab offer direct connectivity to Dharamshala through various means of travel like as follows:

  1. Road:

    2. There are multiple public and private buses available from the varying parts of Delhi, Punjab, and Himachal. Travellers can book Volvo, Deluxe, Semi-Deluxe, or ordinary bus service from a particular region to reach Dharamshala either in the daytime or a night. Even travellers can reach Dharamshala using their private four-wheelers or two-wheeler by taking regular halts at places. The road condition from Delhi to Dharamshala is smooth unless traveling in the rainy season.

  2. Rail:

    3. There is no direct train to Dharamshala for travellers coming from various parts of India. The nearest railway station is Pathankot which is located 85km away from Dharamshala. Regular taxi and bus service is available from Pathankot to Dharamshala.

  3. Air:

    4. The nearest airport to Dharamshala is at Gaggal which is 13km away from the city. The airport is directly connected to major cities like Delhi, Jaipur, Mumbai, Kolkata, Pune, and Hyderabad. From the airport, travellers can easily get a cab from there to Dharamshala or Mcleodganj city.

Best Time to Visit

Mid-February to mid-July is the best time of year to visit Dharamshala because of the beautiful spring and summer months, when temperatures range from 21 to 34 degrees Celsius. Winters bring freezing temperatures and unpredictable snowfall, but they also add to the beauty of the valley.
