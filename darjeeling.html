About

The windy lanes of Darjeeling is often peppered with “Aiya! Kay saro chisso hau!” (Gosh, why is it so cold!) as people hunch over small fires outside shops and at street corners. A collective excitement ripples through when the sun comes out: people hurry to spread out blankets, carpets, and pickles on their roofs and balcony railings. If there is a common thread that binds all the individuals within Darjeeling (apart from their love for the Chowrastha, long walks around the Mall or gossip over hot tea), it is their love for the off-duty sun, usually masked by clouds or robbed by the cold winds that blow across the Eastern Himalayas.

Darjeeling has seen a long and conflicting political and social journey of its own. It has been home to several temporary and permanent homes. Shaped by the people and their histories, no one history reigns supreme, nor one grand narrative summarises the heterogeneity of the aspirations and the culture that lives within the town. It is the sound of the Toy Train, the gongs from the Japanese temple, the hustle and bustle of Chowk Bazar, as well as the constant utterance of “dus rupey, dus rupey, dus rupey” by second-hand sellers as we climb “Orient ko ukkalo” that make its soundtrack hard to define, hard to box in.

  3. Ghoom Monastery

    4. Ghoom Monastery, also known as Yiga Choeling Monastery, is one of the oldest Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in the region. Housing a 15-feet high statue of Maitreya Buddha, the monastery is a serene place for meditation and reflection and a must-visit among the famous places in Darjeeling to visit.

    • Location: Ghoom, Darjeeling
    • Entry Fee: Free
    • Timings: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM
    • Distance from Darjeeling Himalayan Railway Station: 7km
    • Time required: 1-2 hours
    • Best for: It is one of the best places to visit in Darjeeling with family.
    • Tip: Respect the religious customs and maintain silence inside the monastery.
    • Nearby Attractions: Tso Lhamo Lake, Katao, Observatory Hill, Mahakal Temple, Mirik Lake
    • Google rating and reviews: 4.3 rating and 8,000 reviews

    How to reach:

    Reaching the magnificent hill station of Darjeeling is not a hassle for travellers coming from various parts of India. Delhi and Punjab offer direct connectivity to Darjeeling through various means of travel like as follows:

    1. Road:

      2. There are multiple public and private buses available from the varying parts of Delhi, Punjab, and Himachal. Travellers can book Volvo, Deluxe, Semi-Deluxe, or ordinary bus service from a particular region to reach Darjeeling either in the daytime or a night. Even travellers can reach Darjeeling using their private four-wheelers or two-wheeler by taking regular halts at places. The road condition from Delhi to Darjeeling is smooth unless traveling in the rainy season.

    2. Rail:

      3. There is no direct train to Darjeeling for travellers coming from various parts of India. The nearest railway station is Pathankot which is located 85km away from Darjeeling. Regular taxi and bus service is available from Pathankot to Darjeeling.

    3. Air:

      4. The nearest airport to Darjeeling is at Gaggal which is 13km away from the city. The airport is directly connected to major cities like Delhi, Jaipur, Mumbai, Kolkata, Pune, and Hyderabad. From the airport, travellers can easily get a cab from there to Darjeeling or Mcleodganj city.
